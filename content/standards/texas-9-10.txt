English I / II — Reading, Strands 4–10
4.A  Establish purpose for reading and monitor comprehension.
5.E  Make connections to personal experiences, ideas in other texts, and society.
6.C  Use text evidence to support an appropriate response.
7.A  Analyze how themes are developed through characterization and plot in fiction.
8.B  Analyze the effect of meter and structural elements such as line breaks in poems.
8.D  Analyze how the author's use of language contributes to mood, voice, and tone.
9.A  Explain the author's purpose and message within a text.
10.A Evaluate the use of figurative language and rhetorical devices.

Composition
11.A Plan a piece of writing appropriate for various purposes and audiences.
11.B Develop drafts into a focused, structured, and coherent piece of writing.
11.D Edit drafts using standard English conventions.

Multimodal
12.B Use an appropriate mode of delivery to present multimodal compositions.
