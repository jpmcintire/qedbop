English III / IV — Reading
4.A  Establish purpose for reading.
5.E  Make connections among texts and across genres, cultures, and historical periods.
6.C  Use text evidence to support an appropriate response, drawing inferences and synthesizing.
7.A  Analyze relationships among themes, conflicts, and the development of characters in literary works.
8.B  Analyze the effect of meter, structural elements, and patterns of sound in poems and dramatic works.
8.D  Analyze how an author's diction and figurative language contribute to voice, tone, and meaning.
9.A  Explain the author's purpose, message, and rhetorical strategies.
10.A Evaluate the use of literary and rhetorical devices in a range of genres.

Composition
11.A Plan and draft a focused, organized piece of writing for various purposes.
11.B Develop drafts into a substantive piece of writing.
11.D Edit drafts using standard English conventions.
